If you're looking for somewhere to stay, you've come to the right place! We have plenty of options for student accommodation in London, where you can find the perfect base for your time in the city. Live in London's best student areas

King's Cross

Ideally placed just a stone's throw from London's main university district, King's Cross is all about convenience. It's home to our Depot Point residence, which is within walking distance of UCL, LSE, Birkbeck and several other major institutions.

Not only that, but you also have the British Library and London's famous Museum Mile right on your doorstep, as well as top-class transport connections that make exploring the city a breeze.

Explore Depot Point

Farringdon

Mixing historic charm with modern appeal, Farringdon is an area that's brimming with character. The location couldn't be much better for students, with City St. George's just up the road from our Therese House residence and UCL, KCL and LSE a short tube ride away.

With its plethora of pubs, bars, restaurants and nightclubs nearby, you'll never tire of new places to try. Alternatively, you can get your culture fix at the Barbican Centre or stroll down to the City for some sightseeing.

Explore Therese House

Whitechapel

Based in London's historic East End, the multicultural neighbourhood of Whitechapel is full of infectious energy. Home to our The Curve residence, it's well placed for reaching QMUL and UCL East, while other major universities are easily accessible via the tube.

Bustling Brick Lane has everything from vibrant street art and vintage clothing to authentic curry houses serving up some of the best South Asian food in London. And for nights out, the student hub of Shoreditch is close by.

The ultimate student playground

London has everything a student could ever need — from green spaces to cultural highlights, historic hotspots to world-class shopping, plus arguably the best social scene in the UK. Our student accommodation in London gives you an address in the heart of the action, with a ton of attractions on your doorstep (or a quick tube hop away!).

With so much to do, boredom is out of the question. Trendy areas such as Shoreditch, Camden, Brixton and Borough offer the perfect student hangout, whether you fancy a drink, a bite to eat or checking out what the markets have to offer. Or hit up London's many amazing parks and free museums for a cheap day out!

World-leading universities

With no less than 40 universities to its name — including many of the world's top-ranking institutions — London may just be the best city on the planet when it comes to universities. The key players include UCL, KCL, Imperial and LSE, while the likes of QMUL, City St George’s, UWL and UEL have massive student populations too.

And that's before we get onto London's specialist universities — institutions like UAL, SOAS and Goldsmiths offer best-in-class courses in their respective fields. You'll find students from all sorts of universities staying at our accommodation, giving you the chance to meet people with diverse interests and backgrounds.

Frequently asked questions

What are the best areas for students to live in London?

Birmingham City University's main campus is based in the Eastside district of Birmingham city centre, just a 15-minute walk from New Street station. The university also has a second campus in Edgbaston, along with various other facilities in the Jewellery Quarter, Digbeth, Perry Barr and Bournville — so it really has a city-wide presence!

Is London a good city for international students?

London is said to have more international students than any city in the world, so you won't find a better place to study abroad! When you consider its wealth of first-class universities, incredible cultural diversity, thriving jobs market and near-infinite range of things to do, it's easy to see why.

What's the cost of living for students in London?

It's no secret that London is an expensive place to live, but costs vary considerably depending on where and how you choose to live. At the lower end of the scale, you may be able to get by with around £1,500 per month, but bigger spenders will likely want to budget at least £2,500 to cover monthly living costs.

How much does student accommodation in London cost?

Accommodation costs vary significantly depending on the area and amenities, with the rule of thumb being that the more central the location, the more expensive the price. It's hard to estimate precise costs, but as a rough estimate, private rents for student accommodation in London typically range from £1,500–£2,000 per month.

What are the best student discounts in London?

Countless attractions in London offer discounted rates to students — there's the London Eye, the London Dungeon, Madame Tussauds and the Tower of London, to name just a few. Platforms like UNiDAYS and Student Beans unlock additional discounts at hundreds of shops, restaurants and leisure destinations throughout the city.
