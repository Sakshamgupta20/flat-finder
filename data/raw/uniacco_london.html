Best Universities In London For International Students

London is home to some of the world’s most prestigious universities, making it a top choice for students worldwide. Pursuing business, tech, arts, or sciences? London has a university that fits your academic goals.

Here are some of the best universities in London:

  1. University College London (UCL) – Consistently ranked among the world’s top 10 universities, UCL excels in research, medicine, and engineering.
  2. King’s College London (KCL) – A globally respected institution known for its excellence in health sciences, law, and business.
  3. Imperial College London – A leader in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) education, renowned for its cutting-edge research.
  4. London School of Economics & Political Science (LSE) – A top-ranked university for economics, finance, and social sciences, attracting students from over 150 countries.
  5. Queen Mary University of London (QMUL) – Known for its medical and engineering programs, with a strong focus on diversity and global impact.
  6. London Business School (LBS) – One of the best business schools in the world, offering top-tier MBA and finance programs.
  7. University of the Arts London (UAL) – The go-to institution for fashion, design, and creative arts, producing industry leaders in the creative sector.

Best Areas for Student Accommodation in London – Where Should You Live?

London is big. Really big! Choosing the right area makes all the difference.

London’s student accommodations are spread across nine transport zones, but most students prefer Zones 1, 2, and 3—a perfect mix of convenience, affordability, and access to top universities.

Zone 1: The heart of London, closest to major universities and cultural hotspots.

Zone 2: A balance between central access and lower rent, ideal for students who want to be close but not in the middle of the city rush.

Zone 3: A budget-friendly option with great transport links and quieter surroundings.

Hotspots You Can't Miss:

  • Brockley Market – A Saturday-only market, cash only!
  • Boxpark Shoreditch – Pop-up restaurants & budget-friendly eats
  • Camden Market – Vintage shopping & amazing street food
  • Maltby Market & Portobello Market – Hidden gems for food lovers
     
  1. Westminster Abbey – A 700-year-old masterpiece near Big Ben. This UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-visit for history buffs.
     
  2. Pins at a Pub – London’s historic pub culture is more than just drinks!
  • Ye Old Cheshire Cheese (Smithfield) – One of the city's most iconic pubs
  • The Churchill Arms (Notting Hill) – Famous for its floral façade & Thai cuisine
     
  1. Hyde Park – The ultimate student chill zone—perfect for a study break or picnic!

 

Best Way to Get Around:

London’s transport system is fast, efficient, and student-friendly—if you know how to use it smartly!

Tube 

  • Peak fares (£4.25-£8.50/day): Mon-Fri (6:30-9:30 AM & 4:00-6:30 PM)
  • Off-peak fares (£1.80 per trip): All other times & public holidays

Buses

  • Flat fare: £1.75 per ride
  • Daily cap: £5.25 for unlimited bus travel
  • Free transfers: Unlimited within one hour of your first tap

Cycling 

  • Santander Cycles: £1.65 per 30 minutes
  • Dedicated cycling lanes across London

Student Travel Discounts to Save on Commute

  • Student Oyster Card – Get 30% off on buses, trams & the Tube.
  • 16-25 Railcard – Save up to 1/3rd on train fares across the UK.
  • National Express Student Card – Discounts on intercity coach travel.

Part-time Job Opportunities for Students:

London has plenty of student-friendly job opportunities that fit around your schedule while helping you earn extra bucks.

  • Retail & Hospitality – Work in cafés, supermarkets, and stores like Tesco, Primark, or Starbucks.
  • Tutoring & Remote Jobs – Teach subjects, freelance, or find online gigs in writing or virtual assistance.
  • On-Campus Jobs – Library assistant, student ambassador, or admin roles—check your university job portal.
  • Internships & Placements – Gain experience in tech, finance, marketing, and media—apply by Term 2.
  • Tier 4 (Student) Visa holders can work up to 20 hours/week during term time & full-time on holidays.
